Reflection

This project was an absolute dream. I spent 5 weeks listening to some of my favorite songs, from one of my favorite movies, made by one of my favorite comedians. I could come home from a stressful day, listen to one of my favorite songs, and call it “doing homework.”

Since I was a teenager, I’ve had an ability to vividly put visuals to audio, so much so that I wrote scripts of music videos for some of my favorite songs, and was legitimately frustrated by most “visualizer” programs because they never seems to get the visuals “quite right.” Throughout the creation of this project, I thought a lot about how to engage with readers. Specifically, what would make things more engaging. I kept coming back to this idea that multimodal writing can be a means through which “felt sense” is conveyed.

This project felt like a way to manifest what I “see” in my mind’s eye onto the screen.

The problems I faced wasn’t coming up with ideas. It was not having computer programs (and more importantly the skills) to make those ideas reality.

I have million dollar ideas, with dollar store skills.
