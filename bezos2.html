Bezos II
Recreator’s Note
As the name implies, this is the second “Bezos” song, consisting of limited, but repeated, lyrics. As with the first Bezos song, “Bezos II” drips with irony and sarcasm celebrating Amazon CEO, Jeffrey Bezos’s success of being the richest person alive, having heavily profited during the COVID-19 pandemic. “Bezos II” appears to be a song that worships Bezos, chanting his name, encouraging and offering congratulations. However, in the context of this comedy special that criticizes capitalist greed, listeners can quickly presume that Burnham is not, in fact, a fan of Jeffrey Bezos. This song is a parody of an "underdog story," meant to make viewers think that Jeffrey Bezos, one of the wealthiest men ever to live, somehow needs encouragement or persuasion to keep fighting for success.

My lyrics are meant to convey the absolutely cheesiest, cringiest ways to format the repeated lyric, “Jeffrey Bezos," to really bring forward the tongue-in-cheekiness. Between the chanting of the name, Burnham inserts gratifying sounds and words of affirmation. While the act of putting together the lyrics for this song was easy, it was particularly difficult to format those lyrics in a very obviously ironic way. I used popular internet memes/gifs that carry with them ironic, even campy, connotations. Using a host of memes seems relevant since Burnham gained his fame from being a YouTube star. Many of fans would appreciate the history and use of memes within the Burnham lyrics.

First, I used the “Feel Good” meme, where an outline of a man touching his face is fraught with ironic satisfaction. Second, admittedly, I altered the star image that usually says, “your did it,” yet again an ironic image because of the “blursed” (a portmanteau of blessed and cursed) star that is terribly wrong, but looks as if someone tried their pitiful best. Third, a gif from Adult Swim’s Children’s Hospital underscores the sarcastic tone of excitement by showing a character throwing confetti up in celebration that his tv show was canceled. Last is word art to highlight the over-the-top-ness by making the last syllable huge and tacky, like the rest of the Jeffrey Bezos gifs.
